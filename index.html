Domain-Specific Quality Estimation for Machine Translation in Low-Resource Scenarios. Built a two-track evaluation system for English→Indic machine translation across Healthcare, Legal, Tourism, and General content. WRITING

Published research

Domain-Specific Quality Estimation for Machine Translation in Low-Resource Scenarios

LoResLM Workshop, EACL 2026 · First author, with A. Ranu, A. Sindhujan, D. Kanojia · arXiv:2603.07372

Studies sentence-level quality estimation for English-to-Indic machine translation across four domains and five language pairs, comparing zero-shot, few-shot, and guideline-anchored prompting on closed- and open-weight LLMs. Closed-weight models do well on prompting alone, but open-weight models stay unreliable in high-risk domains — which motivates ALOPE, a LoRA-based adaptation approach with regression heads on intermediate transformer layers, extended here with Low-Rank Multiplicative Adaptation (LoRMA).
